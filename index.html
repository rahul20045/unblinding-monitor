Context

What is Unblinding?

Definition

Unblinding is the premature revelation of a participant's assigned treatment arm (active drug vs. placebo) to any party who should remain masked — including investigators, study staff, or outcome assessors.

Why It Matters

Even a single unblinding event can introduce systematic bias, compromise endpoint integrity, and trigger regulatory findings. It may lead to data exclusion, trial suspension, or rejection of the NDA/BLA submission.

Common Causes

  • 01Distinguishable dosage forms
  • 02AESI pattern recognition
  • 03Laboratory value leakage
  • 04Third-party data access errors

Process

Unblinding Response Workflow

Detection & Alert
Automated triggers from IWRS, eCRF data patterns, site staff reports, or DSMB referrals. SLA: immediate notification to unblinding custodian.
T+0 hours
Preliminary Assessment
Scope the affected subjects, verify blinding status in the system, classify as confirmed accidental vs. suspected, and document initial findings.
T+24 hours
Impact Analysis
Evaluate bias risk for primary/secondary endpoints, assess affected data sets, perform sensitivity analysis excluding unblinded subjects.
T+72 hours
CAPA & Closure
Implement corrective actions, update SAP if needed, notify regulatory authorities if threshold exceeded, close with documented rationale.
T+14 days
Critical Escalation Trigger
If unblinding rate exceeds 1.0% of enrolled subjects, or if any single site has >2 events, automatic escalation to the DSMB Chair and Sponsor Medical Director within 4 hours.
Documentation Requirements
Each event requires: source documentation, root cause analysis (5-Why), affected subject list, impact assessment form, and CAPA plan — all filed within 7 calendar days.
